About This Initiative

Our first environmental activity of the summer is aimed at addressing the critical challenges facing our local ecosystem. With climate change accelerating and pollution reaching new heights, it's more important than ever to take direct action in our communities.

This initiative will focus on three main areas:

  • Cleaning up local waterways that have been contaminated by industrial waste
  • Planting native species to restore habitats damaged by urban development
  • Educational workshops for community members on sustainable living practices

Your donation will directly fund the purchase of cleanup equipment, native plants, and educational materials. We've already secured commitments from local businesses to match donations up to $2,000, which means your contribution will have double the impact!

Last year, our summer environmental activities resulted in the removal of over 2 tons of waste from local waterways and the planting of more than 500 native trees and shrubs. This year, with your help, we aim to double those numbers.

The program will run for 8 weeks, beginning June 1st and ending July 31st. Volunteers of all ages are welcome to participate, and no prior experience is necessary. We provide all training and equipment needed.

How Your Donation Helps

$25 - Provides cleanup supplies for one volunteer
$50 - Plants 5 native trees or shrubs
$100 - Funds an educational workshop for 20 community members
$250 - Sponsors a full day of waterway cleanup
$500 - Restores approximately 1/4 acre of damaged habitat

Thank you for your support in making our environment cleaner, healthier, and more sustainable for future generations!
